excerpt: "Mathematic models of epidemics are the key tool for predicting future course of disease in a population and analyzing the effects of possible intervention policies. Typically, models that produce deterministic are applied for making predictions and reaching decisions. Stochastic modeling methods present an alternative. Here, we demonstrate by example why it is important that stochastic modeling be used in population health decision support systems."
