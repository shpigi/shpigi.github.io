excerpt: "Using fMRI imaging, we tracked brain responses while participants listened to music and later reported emotional experiences on valence and arousal scales. The findings reveal an association between the unfolding music-induced emotionality and dorsal caudate/amygdala (DCA) modulation within a vast network of limbic regions. Musical beat-strength and tempo mediated this limbic response. A left fronto-parietal network showed emotional associations only among musically experienced participants (>5 years), suggesting two distinct processing levels: a shared core-affective limbic process and an experience-dependent process potentially involving mirror neurons."
