excerpt: "We present a real-time method to accurately demarcate subterritories of the subthalamic nucleus (STN) during surgery, based on microelectrode recordings (MERs) and a Hidden Markov Model (HMM). Using data from 56 trajectories across 21 Parkinson's disease patients undergoing bilateral surgery, Root Mean Square and Power Spectral Density measurements were used to train the algorithm. The HMM successfully identified key anatomical boundaries with high accuracy, achieving detection reliability (error < 1 mm) of 95, 86, and 91% for different demarcation points, demonstrating improved precision compared to previously established automated methods."
